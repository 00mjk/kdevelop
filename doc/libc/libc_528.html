Implementing a Job Control Shell

This section describes what a shell must do to implement job control, by presenting an extensive sample program to illustrate the concepts involved.

  • section Data Structures for the Shell, introduces the example and presents its primary data structures.
  • section Initializing the Shell, discusses actions which the shell must perform to prepare for job control.
  • section Launching Jobs, includes information about how to create jobs to execute commands.
  • section Foreground and Background, discusses what the shell should do differently when launching a job in the foreground as opposed to a background job.
  • section Stopped and Terminated Jobs, discusses reporting of job status back to the shell.
  • section Continuing Stopped Jobs, tells you how to continue jobs that have been stopped.
  • section The Missing Pieces, discusses other parts of the shell.
